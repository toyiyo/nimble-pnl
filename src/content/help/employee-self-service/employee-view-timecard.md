# View Your Timecard and Hours

The **My Timecard** page gives you a week-by-week view of every punch you have recorded — including your net hours after breaks, regular hours, overtime, and a day-by-day breakdown of each individual clock-in, clock-out, break start, and break end.

## Before you begin

- You must be signed in to EasyShiftHQ with a staff or chef account.
- Your account must be linked to an employee record at your restaurant. If you see an **Access Required** or similar message, contact your manager — they need to connect your login to your employee profile before the timecard is available.

---

## Go to My Timecard

1. In the navigation menu, tap **My Timecard**. The **My Timecard** page opens.
2. Your name and position appear in the page header beneath the title.

---

## Choose the week you want to view

At the top of the page, a period selector shows the date range of the currently displayed week. You can change the period in two ways:

**Use the period dropdown** to jump directly to a preset range:
- **Current Week** — the week in progress right now.
- **Last Week** — the previous full week.
- **Custom Week** — lets you navigate to any specific week using the arrow buttons.

**Use the arrow and Today buttons** to step through weeks one at a time:
- Tap the **left arrow** to move to the previous week.
- Tap **Today** (in the centre, between the arrows) to jump straight back to the current week.
- Tap the **right arrow** to move to the next week.

---

## Read the four summary cards

Below the period selector, four summary cards display your totals for the selected week at a glance.

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Net Hours** | Your total worked hours after all break time has been subtracted. |
| **Regular Hours** | The portion of your net hours up to 40 hours per week. |
| **Overtime** | Any net hours beyond 40 hours in the week. Overtime appears in amber (orange) when it is greater than zero. |
| **Break Time** | The total time you spent on all breaks during the week. |

All totals are displayed in hours and minutes (for example, **8h 30m**).

---

## Read the Daily Breakdown

Scroll down to the **Daily Breakdown** section. Each day of the selected week is listed with:

- The day name and date (for example, **Monday May 25**).
- A **Today** badge on the current day's row.
- The net hours worked that day, shown on the right side of the row.
- If you took a break that day, the total break time appears just below the net hours in smaller text.
- All individual punches for that day, shown as small rounded badges in chronological order. Each badge contains an icon and the exact time of the punch:
  - A green arrow-in icon for a **clock-in** punch.
  - A red arrow-out icon for a **clock-out** punch.
  - A coffee-cup icon for a **break start** punch.
  - A play-circle icon for a **break end** punch.

If no punches were recorded for a particular day, the row shows **No punches recorded**.

---

## Identify overtime hours

Overtime is any net time you worked beyond 40 hours in the week. When your weekly total exceeds 40 hours, the **Overtime** summary card displays the extra hours in **amber (orange)** text. A value of **0h 0m** (shown in normal text) means you have not exceeded 40 hours for that week.

---

## Contact your manager about discrepancies

If a punch looks wrong — a missing clock-out, an incorrect time, or a punch you do not recognize — you cannot edit your own timecard. Contact your manager and ask them to correct the record. Only approved punches are included in payroll calculations.

---

## Tips

- **Use the arrows to review past weeks.** You can navigate as far back as your punch history goes — there is no limit on how many previous weeks you can check.
- **Today's row is highlighted** with a subtle background so it is easy to spot in the daily list.
- **Punches appear in the order they were recorded.** If you clocked in, started a break, ended the break, and then clocked out, you will see four badges in that order for the day.
- **Net Hours vs. Regular Hours.** Net Hours is always your total worked time minus breaks. Regular Hours is capped at 40 — once Net Hours exceeds 40, Regular Hours stays at 40h 0m and the rest moves into Overtime.

## Troubleshooting

**I see an "Access Required" or similar message and cannot view the timecard.**
Your login is not linked to an employee profile. Ask your manager to connect your account to your employee record.

**A punch is missing or shows the wrong time.**
You cannot edit punches yourself. Contact your manager — they can correct the record from the manager time-punches view.

**The Overtime card shows amber hours but I did not think I worked that much.**
The app counts all approved punch time toward the weekly total, including time from multiple days. Check the Daily Breakdown to see which days contributed the most hours. If the total still seems wrong, speak with your manager.

**The page shows "No punches recorded" for every day of a week I know I worked.**
Make sure you are viewing the correct week using the period selector arrows. If you are on the right week and the punches are still missing, contact your manager — they may need to investigate or manually enter the records.

## Frequently asked questions

**How far back can I look at my timecard?**
You can use the left arrow to navigate through as many past weeks as your punch history allows. There is no limit displayed in the app.

**Does the timecard show unapproved or flagged punches?**
The timecard shows all punches recorded for your account. However, only approved punches are counted toward payroll. If you notice a discrepancy, contact your manager.

**What counts as overtime?**
Any net hours (hours worked minus break time) that exceed 40 in a single week are treated as overtime and appear highlighted in amber on the Overtime card.

**Why is Break Time shown separately from Net Hours?**
Net Hours already has break time subtracted. The **Break Time** card is there so you can see exactly how much time was recorded as breaks during the week, separate from your worked time.

**Can I correct my own punches?**
No. Only managers and owners can edit or add punches. If you spot an error, let your manager know and they can fix it from the time-punches manager view.
