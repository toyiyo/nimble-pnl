# Set Your Availability and Request Time Off

The **Employee Portal** is where you tell your manager when you are available to work and when you need time off. You can set a recurring weekly pattern, flag specific dates that are different from your normal schedule, and submit time-off requests — all in one place.

## Before you begin

- You must be signed in to EasyShiftHQ with a staff or chef account.
- Your account must be linked to an employee record at your restaurant. If you see an **Access Required** message along with a prompt to contact your manager, your login has not yet been connected to your employee profile. Reach out to your manager before continuing.

---

## Go to the Employee Portal

1. Navigate to **/employee/portal** in the app.
2. The **Employee Portal** page opens and greets you by name.
3. You will see two tabs at the top: **Time Off** and **Availability**. The **Time Off** tab is shown by default.

---

## Set your regular weekly availability

Your weekly availability tells your manager which days and times you are generally free to work each week.

1. From the Employee Portal, click the **Availability** tab.
2. In the **Weekly Availability** section, click **Add Availability**.
3. The **Set Availability** dialog opens. Fill in the following fields:
   - **Day of Week** — choose the day this entry applies to (Sunday through Saturday).
   - **Is available on this day** — the toggle is on by default (meaning you are available). Turn it off if you are unavailable on this day entirely.
   - **Start Time** and **End Time** — enter the window you are available. These fields appear only when the toggle is on.
   - **Notes (Optional)** — add any extra context, such as "prefer morning shifts only."
4. Click **Save** to add the entry.

Repeat this process for each day of the week you want to specify. Days you do not add an entry for have no stated preference on file.

To update an existing weekly entry, click the **pencil icon** next to the row you want to change, make your edits in the dialog that opens, and click **Update**.

---

## Add an availability exception for a specific date

Availability exceptions let you override your regular weekly pattern for a single date — for example, if you can only work a shortened shift on a particular day, or you are completely unavailable on a holiday.

1. From the **Availability** tab, scroll down to the **Availability Exceptions** section.
2. Click **Add Exception**.
3. The **New Availability Exception** dialog opens. Fill in the following fields:
   - **Date** — click **Pick a date** to open a calendar and select the specific date.
   - **Available on this date** — the toggle is off by default (meaning you are unavailable on that date). Turn it on if you are available but with different hours than usual.
   - **Start Time** and **End Time** — enter the window you are available on that date. These fields appear only when the toggle is on.
   - **Reason (Optional)** — enter a short note, such as "doctor's appointment" or "holiday."
4. Click **Save** to add the exception.

Exceptions always override your regular weekly availability for that specific date.

To edit an existing exception, click the **pencil icon** next to it, update the details, and click **Update**.

---

## Request time off

1. From the Employee Portal, click the **Time Off** tab (it opens by default when you first arrive).
2. Click **New Request**.
3. The **New Time-Off Request** dialog opens. Fill in:
   - **Start Date** — click **Pick date** to open a calendar and choose the first day of your time off.
   - **End Date** — click **Pick date** to choose the last day. The end date must be on or after the start date.
   - **Reason (Optional)** — type a short explanation if you would like to share one with your manager.
4. Click **Submit Request**.

Your request is saved and appears in the **My Time-Off Requests** list with a **Pending** badge. Your manager will review it and either approve or reject it.

---

## Edit or cancel a pending time-off request

You can only change a request while it still shows a **Pending** status. Approved and rejected requests are read-only.

**To edit a pending request:**
1. Find the request in the **My Time-Off Requests** list.
2. Click the **pencil icon** next to it.
3. Update the start date, end date, or reason in the **Edit Time-Off Request** dialog.
4. Click **Update Request**.

**To cancel a pending request:**
1. Find the request in the **My Time-Off Requests** list.
2. Click the **trash icon** next to it.
3. A confirmation dialog appears asking **Delete Time-Off Request?** with a warning that this action cannot be undone.
4. Click **Delete** to permanently remove the request, or **Cancel** to go back.

## Tips

- **Exceptions win over weekly availability.** If you have a weekly entry that says you are available on Mondays but you add an exception marking a specific Monday as unavailable, your manager sees you as unavailable on that date.
- **You can be unavailable without specifying times.** When you turn the availability toggle off, no time fields are required — the entry simply records that you are unavailable that day or date.
- **Approved and rejected requests are informational only.** Once a manager has reviewed your request, the edit and delete icons are no longer shown. If you need to change something after approval, contact your manager directly.
- **Setting availability is not the same as requesting time off.** Availability tells your manager your general schedule preferences for ongoing scheduling. Time-off requests are for specific date ranges when you need to be away completely.

## Troubleshooting

**I see "Access Required" and cannot use the portal.**
Your account is not yet linked to an employee record. Contact your manager and ask them to connect your login to your employee profile.

**The Save or Submit Request button stays grayed out.**
Make sure all required fields are filled in. For availability, you must select a day of the week. For time-off requests, both a start date and an end date are required, and the end date cannot be earlier than the start date. If you have start and end times entered, the start time must be earlier than the end time.

**I cannot edit or delete my time-off request.**
Only **Pending** requests can be edited or deleted. Once a manager has approved or rejected a request, it becomes read-only. Contact your manager if you need a change made after they have reviewed it.

**My availability exception is not showing the time fields.**
The **Start Time** and **End Time** fields appear only when the **Available on this date** toggle is turned on. If the toggle is off, the exception records you as unavailable for the entire day and no time range is needed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does my manager get notified when I submit a time-off request?**
The request immediately appears in your manager's scheduling view with a **Pending** status. Whether your manager receives a push or email notification depends on their notification settings.

**Can I set availability for the same day more than once?**
You can add multiple entries for the same day of the week — for example, to show two separate available windows. Each entry appears as its own row in the Weekly Availability list.

**What happens if my time-off request overlaps with a shift I am already scheduled for?**
Submitting a time-off request does not automatically remove existing shifts. Your manager is responsible for adjusting the schedule after approving your request. If you are concerned, let your manager know when you submit the request.

**Can I add an exception to mark myself as available on a day I normally said I am unavailable?**
Yes. Exceptions override your weekly availability in both directions — you can use them to mark yourself available on a day you normally listed as unavailable, or unavailable on a day you normally listed as available.

**Who can see my availability and time-off requests?**
Your manager and any owner-level users at your restaurant can see your entries. Other staff members cannot view your personal availability or requests.
