# View Your Pay Estimate for a Pay Period

The **My Pay** page shows you a running estimate of what you have earned for any pay period — including wages, overtime, and tips — so you always know where you stand before payroll is processed.

## Before you begin

Your account must be linked to an employee record in EasyShiftHQ. If you see a message saying your account is not linked, contact your manager.

## Navigate to My Pay

1. Sign in to EasyShiftHQ and select your restaurant if prompted.
2. In the left navigation, open the employee section and tap **My Pay**. The page URL is `/employee/pay`.
3. Your name and pay rate appear in the subtitle below the page title (for example, "Alex Rivera • $18.00/hr" or "Alex Rivera • Salary").

## Choose a pay period

The **Pay Period** selector at the top of the page controls which dates are shown.

1. Click the dropdown next to the **Pay Period:** label.
2. Choose one of the preset options:
   - **Current Week** — the week in progress right now.
   - **Last Week** — the previous full week.
   - **Last 2 Weeks** — the most recent two-week span.
   - **Custom Period** — use the left and right arrow buttons to shift the date range back or forward. Click **Today** to jump back to the current week.
3. The selected date range is displayed in a badge next to the navigation buttons (for example, "May 19 - May 25, 2026").

## Read the summary cards

Four cards at the top of the page give you an at-a-glance view of your earnings for the selected period.

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Hours Worked** | Total hours for the period, with overtime hours listed below if any applied. Shown only for hourly employees. |
| **Days Worked** | Shown instead of Hours Worked if you are paid a daily rate. |
| **Pay Period** | Shown instead of Hours Worked if you are paid a salary or are a contractor. A dash appears in the value — hours are not tracked for these compensation types. |
| **Gross Wages** | Your total wages before taxes. |
| **Tips Earned** | The total tips credited to you. If some tips were already paid to you in cash, the cash amount appears below the total. |
| **Total Pay** | Gross wages plus any tips still owed to you through payroll. |

## Review the Pay Breakdown

Scroll down to the **Pay Breakdown** section for a line-by-line view of how your earnings were calculated.

### Wages

The wages rows shown depend on your compensation type:

- **Regular Pay** (hourly employees) — your regular hours multiplied by your hourly rate.
- **Overtime Pay** (hourly employees) — shown only when you worked more than the overtime threshold. Overtime is calculated at 1.5 times your regular rate and highlighted in amber.
- **Daily Rate Pay** (daily-rate employees) — the number of days you worked multiplied by your daily rate.
- **Salary** — a prorated amount for the selected pay period.
- **Contract Pay** (contractors) — a prorated amount for the selected pay period.

### Tips

If you earned tips during the period, the Tips section appears below Wages:

- **Tips Earned** — the full amount credited to you.
- **Paid in Cash** — shown if any of those tips were already handed to you as a cash payout before payroll. This amount is subtracted from what payroll still owes you.
- **Owed in Payroll** — the remaining tip amount that will be included in your next paycheck.

### Total Compensation

At the bottom of the breakdown, **Total Compensation** repeats your overall total — wages plus any tips remaining in payroll.

## Check for incomplete shift warnings

If the amber warning banner appears at the top of the page, one or more of your shifts for this period is incomplete (for example, a missing clock-out punch). The banner lists up to three specific issues and includes the message:

> "Contact your manager to fix these before payroll is processed."

Reach out to your manager as soon as possible so the shifts can be corrected before payroll runs. Incomplete shifts may cause your pay estimate to be lower than your actual earnings.

## Tips

- The estimate updates automatically. If your manager adds or corrects a time punch, refresh the page and the figures will recalculate within about 30 seconds.
- Switch between pay periods freely — your data is never deleted by changing the date range.
- If you see **No Pay Data**, there are no recorded time punches for the selected period. Check that you chose the correct week, then contact your manager if you believe punches are missing.

## Troubleshooting

**The page shows "Your account is not linked."**
Your login has not been connected to an employee record. Ask your manager to link your account in the employee settings.

**I do not see the Tips section.**
The Tips section only appears when tips have been recorded for you during the selected period. If you expected tips and they are missing, ask your manager to verify that tip splits for those days have been approved.

**My Total Pay looks lower than expected.**
Check the incomplete shift warning. If a shift is listed there, the hours for that shift are not included in the estimate until the shift is fixed. Also confirm you selected the correct pay period.

**The Overtime Pay row is missing even though I worked extra hours.**
Overtime is calculated based on your restaurant's overtime rules. If you believe you worked enough hours to qualify, contact your manager.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is this my actual paycheck amount?**
No. The note at the bottom of the page states: "This is an estimate based on your time punches. Final pay may differ based on manager adjustments, tax withholdings, and other deductions." Use this page to get a general idea of your earnings, not as your official pay statement.

**Why does my Pay Period card show a dash?**
Salary and contractor employees are not tracked by hours. Instead of an Hours Worked card, you see a Pay Period card with a dash — hours do not apply to your compensation type.

**What does "paid in cash" mean under Tips Earned?**
If your manager recorded a cash tip payout to you during the pay period, that amount is shown as already paid. It is deducted from the Tips Earned total, and only the remaining balance — shown as **Owed in Payroll** — will be included in your paycheck.

**Can I see pay from more than two weeks ago?**
Yes. Select **Custom Period** from the Pay Period dropdown and use the left arrow button to navigate back to any earlier week.

**What should I do if I think my pay is wrong?**
First, check the **Pay Breakdown** section to see how each amount was calculated. If something looks incorrect — wrong hours, missing tips, or an unexpected rate — contact your manager. They can correct time punches or tip entries, and your estimate will update once they save the changes.
