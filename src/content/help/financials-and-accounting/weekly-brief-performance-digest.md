# Read Your Weekly Performance Brief

Every Monday morning, EasyShiftHQ automatically compiles the previous week's key numbers into a single page — the **Weekly Brief** — so you can walk in knowing exactly how the week performed and what to tackle first.

## Before you begin

The Weekly Brief is available to **owners** and **managers**. If you do not see it in your navigation, check with your account owner to confirm your role has access.

## Open the Weekly Brief

1. In the main navigation, click **Weekly Brief**.
2. The page loads the most recent completed week automatically. The date range for that week is shown near the top of the page (for example, "May 18 – 24, 2026").

## Browse previous weeks

1. To go back one week, click the **left chevron arrow** ( < ) beside the date range.
2. To go forward one week, click the **right chevron arrow** ( > ) beside the date range.
3. The right arrow is automatically disabled when you are already viewing the most recent week — you cannot navigate into the future.

## Read the four metric cards

Near the top of the page, four cards display the week's headline numbers at a glance:

| Card | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Revenue** | Total net revenue for the week, in dollars |
| **Food Cost %** | Food cost as a percentage of revenue |
| **Labor Cost %** | Labor cost as a percentage of revenue |
| **Prime Cost %** | Combined food and labor cost as a percentage of revenue |

Below each value you will see a small trend arrow and a percentage change labeled **vs prior week**:

- A **green arrow** means the change is favorable (revenue up, or a cost percentage down).
- A **red arrow** means the change is unfavorable (revenue down, or a cost percentage up).

If a metric is flat compared to the prior week, no arrow or percentage is shown.

## Review the "What Changed" section

The **What Changed** section highlights metrics that moved significantly compared to the prior week. Each entry shows:

- A colored badge:
  - **Critical** (red) — a large or high-priority swing that needs prompt attention.
  - **Warning** (amber) — a notable change worth monitoring.
- The metric name (for example, "Food Cost %").
- The current value and the percentage change versus the prior week.

If no metrics crossed a significant threshold, this section does not appear.

## Read the AI narrative in "Summary"

The **Summary** section contains an AI-generated paragraph explaining what drove the week's performance. It synthesizes the numbers into plain-language context so you understand the story behind the metrics, not just the figures.

Below the narrative you will see a **Generated** timestamp showing when the brief was produced.

## Act on the "Top Actions" section

The **Top Actions** section lists up to three prioritized recommendations based on the week's data. Each card shows:

- A **title** — a short description of the recommended action.
- A **description** — more detail on why the action matters.
- An **Impact** badge — indicates how much acting on this recommendation could improve your results.
- An **Effort** badge — indicates how much work the action requires.

Use the Impact and Effort badges together to decide what to tackle first. High Impact, Low Effort items are generally the best starting point.

## Go to the Ops Inbox for open issues

If there are unresolved alerts from the week, an **Open Issues** card appears at the bottom of the page. It shows the total number of open items and, if any are flagged critical, a count of those as well.

Click the **Open Issues** card to jump directly to the **Ops Inbox**, where you can review and act on each alert.

## Tips

- Briefs are generated automatically every Monday morning — there is nothing you need to trigger manually.
- Use the chevron arrows to compare the same metrics across multiple weeks and spot trends over time.
- The Impact and Effort badges on Top Actions are there to help you prioritize when you have limited time — focus on high-impact, low-effort items first.
- If the Ops Inbox shows critical items, address those before the end of the day to prevent small issues from compounding.

## Troubleshooting

**The page shows "No brief generated for this week"**
Briefs are generated every Monday morning. If it is still early Monday or something interrupted the process, check back later in the day. If the problem persists past Monday, contact your account owner.

**The right arrow is grayed out and I cannot click forward.**
This is expected. The right arrow is disabled when you are already on the most recent completed week. Future weeks have no data yet.

**The "What Changed" section does not appear.**
No metrics crossed a significant threshold that week. This means performance was relatively stable compared to the prior week — that is generally a good sign.

**The "Summary" section is blank.**
The AI narrative may not have been generated for that week. Navigate to a more recent week to confirm the feature is working. If the current week's brief is also missing a summary, contact support.

**The "Open Issues" card does not appear.**
There are no open alerts in the Ops Inbox for your restaurant. No action is needed.

## Frequently asked questions

**When exactly is the brief generated?**
Briefs are generated automatically every Monday morning. The exact time can vary slightly, but the brief will be available for the most recent completed week by the time you start your day.

**Does the brief cover the full calendar week?**
The brief covers a rolling seven-day window. The date range displayed at the top of the page (for example, "May 18 – 24, 2026") shows the exact days included.

**How far back can I go using the chevron arrows?**
You can navigate back week by week as far as historical briefs exist for your restaurant. If a week has no brief — for example, weeks before the feature was enabled — you will see "No brief generated for this week."

**What does "Prime Cost %" mean?**
Prime cost is the combination of your food cost and your labor cost. Expressed as a percentage of revenue, it is one of the most important indicators of a restaurant's financial health. A lower prime cost percentage generally means stronger profitability.

**Can I receive the brief by email?**
Email delivery settings are managed in your notification preferences. See [Notification and Email Preferences](/help/notification-and-email-preferences) for instructions.
